Brief

This year, I was asked to create a website for the Wellington Speaking Union (WSU), an organisation that runs public speaking and debating tournaments for students.
Their old website was outdated, and Brooke, the current President of WSU wanted a redone site for their use.
Before creating the website, Brooke and I needed to collaborate and discuss what this website would be used for, and what we wanted to achieve out of its creation. From this point, we created a list of our key aims that would allow Brooke to be as involved in the process, and in providing feedback throughout this project.
In any website, it is important that UX studies are conducted so that the website fits the needs of not only the owner, but also the consumers who will be using and navigating the website.

  • I contacted and interviewed four categories of users who would be accessing and interacting with the website. I wanted to find out their frustrations with the old site, and their expectations for the design and the active elements of the site.
  • I also did competition analysis to start having an understanding of what needed to be included in this site and what other similar websites had used in terms of design and the features that they included. This also enables me to think about both what their site is lacking, and doing a good job in to help me when choosing what would work for this site.
  • Finally, completing a project backlog to have a better grasp on the "big picture" idea for this site but evidently, this is just a simple plan with none of the specific details of features and designs included as these are subject to change.

Relevant Implications Databases

  1. Privacy
    Unfortunately, attention has not been focused on the privacy aspect of this site. However, this is not of huge concern to us at this moment because the databases are unable to be directly accessed by any users of the site. Using the shortcut http:..../data in the search bar will not take users to the data folder. In the case where the builder (myself) or any other admins are able to access the data folder, all passwords are censored out. Additionally, the site is not built with the intention of collecting much personal or valuable data from each of the users as there is no need or use for that. Therefore, any data that is collected by the site should not need to be of any concern to the users. The maximum amount of data that is collected is password, and email - both of which are not sources of compromising information such as an address, age, school etc would be. We also have not done much about privacy of persons displayed in our photos. The current photos in the site as of this moment were taken and displayed with permission, and it will be up to the administrators to take down photos wherein the person photographed no longer consents to having their image displayed on the page. Photos that are attached to (eg) an upcoming news item, or past event which are connected to databases have the ability to be removed as part of the "edit" function on these specific databases.

  3. Sustainability and Future Proofing
    The database should be easily maintained by the administrator, and any edits that need to be made are central to only one area. This isn't true for database tables such as news where other tables like member needs to be referenced with a foreign key, or with comments, where two foreign keys - user and news are both referred to. Therefore, if any changes are made to the member table, then all tables that reference this member table with a foreign key will automatically adjust with the changes instead of needing to manually change each member within each table. For the creation of the 'draw', I initially planned to have three different tables - grade, round, and finally the draw - however, this excessive and in order to reduce resources and to make it easier to maintain everything, I condensed everything into one table. In this situation, the grade, round and draw did not have to be separate entities, and could still effectively operate in one table.

  4. End User Considerations
    The data in the database, and ability to enter, delete and edit data should be queried and displayed in a way that is easy for users to understand and to use. Many of the users in the UX interviews preferred designs that were simple and modern, with not too much block text. Therefore, the styling and forms that have been created so that users can interact with the databases is designed in this way. Additionally, all of these forms have catches that prevent users from submitting forms with incorrect (e.g. not putting in a valid email address in the sign-up form) or impartial information (e.g. when submitting a post to the upcoming events section or recent news). Although most of the design elements in the site have consideration of viewers who are using different devices (e.g. a mobile phone), I have not necessarily considered these varying screen sizes when it comes to the design and styling of the forms thus far. Whilst they should still be usable, they may not be to the standard that is comfortable for all types of users.
    14
  5. Usability
    In the end, I think that usability is not necessarily maximised for all types of users. With the resources available, they have primarily been optimised for a user on a laptop or computer, with significant efforts made to adjust design elements and features for mobile users as well. This may mean that some features like entering a post may be difficult for users on mobile devices which is a flaw of this site. However, the website is broadly easy to navigate with a sticky nav bar that allows users to immediately navigate to other pages if they cannot find the information that they need, or if they clicked something wrong.

Initial Designs

For the WSU site, I first started to plan out what database tables I wanted to include. This also helps me to keep track of where foreign keys should be used instead of an entirely new identity, and also to examine how the tables all interconnect and interact with each other. The first table that I needed to include was the member table as this impacts other tables.

  • On the news page, there are three tables - comments, recent news and upcoming events. Therefore, this is the page where most of the database activity is occurring. Since WSU is an organisation that arranges debates and public speaking competitions, recent news and upcoming events will be key features of the site and therefore, should be prioritised. This allows all four groups of expected users: teachers, students, coaches as well as judges to be able to keep up with events that they can attend, and what happened at those various competitions. For the administrators, or other authorised users, this will be the hub for keeping those users up-to-date, and it should be easy for them to use the forms to post, edit and delete various news items.
  • Another useful database that is not set in the "news" page is the draw table. This was a popular demand from the interviews conducted in the planning stage. Many students and coaches like to keep up to date with who their teams will be debating, and also to look back on what debates have already happened.

               from news
               join member on news.member_id= member.member_id
               order by news.newsdate desc;
               """
            result = run_search_query_tuples(sql, (), db_path, True)
            print(result)
            return render_template("news.html", news=result)
